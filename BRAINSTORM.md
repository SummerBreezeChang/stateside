# Brainstorm Session

Use this as a 30–45 minute working session. Favor a narrow, memorable product with one excellent end-to-end flow over a broad platform.

## 1. Start with lived problems

Write short answers without judging them yet.

- What repetitive task annoyed you this week?
- What information do you repeatedly translate, summarize, reconcile, or chase?
- Where do people lose context between tools?
- What task requires judgment but still has a checkable outcome?
- What would make a compelling before/after demo in 90 seconds?
- What useful workflow becomes possible specifically because GPT-5.6 can reason, use tools, or work across modalities?

## 2. Generate candidates

For each problem, complete this sentence:

> For **[specific user]** who struggles with **[specific recurring problem]**, **[project]** turns **[input]** into **[valuable outcome]** by **[distinct mechanism]**.

Create at least six candidates: two safe, two ambitious, and two strange-but-plausible.

## 3. Apply the hackathon filter

Reject or shrink ideas that cannot answer yes to most of these:

- Can one person build the core loop before the deadline?
- Can a judge understand the problem in ten seconds?
- Is there a visible transformation or result to demo?
- Is GPT-5.6 essential rather than decorative?
- Is Codex's contribution to the build concrete and documentable?
- Can judges test it without special hardware, paid access, or rebuilding it?
- Does it feel like a coherent product rather than a technical proof of concept?
- Is the data safe, licensed, and easy to provide as a sample?

## 4. Pick finalists

Move the best three ideas to `IDEA_SCORECARD.md`. For each finalist, define:

- **Hero flow:** the single path the demo must show
- **Magic moment:** the surprising or unusually useful result
- **Proof:** how a judge can tell the output is good
- **Fallback:** the smaller version that is still shippable
- **Non-goals:** features deliberately excluded this week

## 5. Commit to one

Choose the idea with the best combination of impact, novelty, product coherence, and achievable implementation—not the largest feature list. Copy it into `PROJECT_BRIEF.md` and begin with the thinnest working vertical slice.
